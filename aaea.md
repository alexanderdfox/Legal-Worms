# Adaptive Audit Escalation Act (AAEA)

## A Bill
To establish a constitutionally compliant, tiered, trigger-based federal audit escalation system designed to detect, investigate, and contain fraud, waste, abuse, and systemic corruption through predefined automatic expansion rules, while preserving separation of powers, due process, and judicial review under the Constitution of the United States.

---

## SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE

This Act may be cited as the **“Adaptive Audit Escalation Act of 2026” (AAEA).**

---

## SECTION 2. PURPOSE

The purpose of this Act is to:
1. Strengthen oversight of federal agencies and federally funded entities;
2. Detect and investigate corruption, fraud, waste, and abuse through structured auditing mechanisms;
3. Enable predefined, rule-based escalation of audits when credible risk indicators are identified;
4. Ensure compliance with the United States Constitution, including due process and separation of powers.

---

## SECTION 3. DEFINITIONS

For purposes of this Act:

**(1) Tier 1 Audit**  
A standard periodic or randomized compliance audit conducted under existing authority of Inspectors General or the Government Accountability Office.

**(2) Tier 2 Audit**  
A targeted, expanded audit triggered by defined risk indicators requiring deeper review of related programs, contracts, and expenditures.

**(3) Tier 3 Audit**  
A systemic network audit extending to all materially connected agencies, contractors, subcontractors, and recipients of federal funds.

**(4) Trigger Event**  
Any condition meeting statutorily defined thresholds indicating elevated risk of fraud, corruption, or systemic noncompliance.

**(5) Audit Network Graph**  
A structured mapping of financial, contractual, and operational relationships between audited entities.

---

## SECTION 4. ESTABLISHMENT OF ADAPTIVE AUDIT SYSTEM

(a) There is established within the federal oversight framework an **Adaptive Audit Escalation System (AAES)**.

(b) The AAES shall operate as a tiered, rule-based escalation structure consisting of Tier 1, Tier 2, and Tier 3 audits.

(c) All escalation rules shall be predefined by statute and may not be altered by administrative discretion except as authorized by Congress.

---

## SECTION 5. TRIGGER EVENTS

(a) A Tier 2 audit shall be automatically initiated upon occurrence of any Trigger Event, including:

1. Statistically significant financial irregularities exceeding thresholds defined by the Government Accountability Office;
2. Verified whistleblower allegations corroborated by independent review;
3. Repeated audit anomalies within the same program or agency;
4. Judicial findings or probable cause determinations related to fraud or statutory violations;
5. Systemic procurement irregularities identified through federal contracting databases.

(b) Trigger Events shall be defined in measurable, objective criteria published in advance.

---

## SECTION 6. TIER 2 EXPANSION REQUIREMENTS

(a) Upon activation of a Tier 2 Audit:
1. The audit scope shall expand to include all related contracts, sub-agencies, and financial flows connected to the triggering entity;
2. Relevant records shall be preserved under mandatory retention order;
3. Historical review shall extend to a minimum of five (5) fiscal years unless otherwise specified by statute.

(b) Tier 2 audits shall remain subject to oversight by Inspectors General and applicable judicial review.

---

## SECTION 7. TIER 3 SYSTEMIC EXPANSION

(a) A Tier 3 Audit shall be triggered if Tier 2 findings demonstrate credible evidence of systemic or networked corruption.

(b) Upon activation of Tier 3:
1. The audit shall expand to all materially connected entities identified in the Audit Network Graph;
2. Expansion shall continue until all statutory boundaries are reached;
3. All linked federal funds, contracts, and transfers shall be included in the audit scope.

(c) Expansion under this section shall occur automatically pursuant to predefined statutory rules and shall not require additional discretionary approval.

---

## SECTION 8. AUDIT NETWORK GRAPH SYSTEM

(a) The Government Accountability Office shall maintain a secure system to map relationships between:
- Federal agencies
- Contractors and subcontractors
- Grant recipients
- Interagency funding pathways

(b) The Audit Network Graph shall be used solely for audit scope determination and shall not constitute a determination of guilt or liability.

---

## SECTION 9. TRANSPARENCY AND REPORTING

(a) All Tier 2 and Tier 3 audits shall produce:
1. A public summary report (appropriately redacted for classified or sensitive information);
2. A detailed congressional briefing;
3. Referral to appropriate Inspectors General or the Department of Justice where legal thresholds are met.

(b) Reports shall be issued on a recurring schedule until audit closure.

---

## SECTION 10. CONSTITUTIONAL SAFEGUARDS

(a) Nothing in this Act shall be construed to:
1. Violate the Fifth Amendment right to due process;
2. Merge investigative and adjudicative authority in a single entity;
3. Expand federal power beyond that granted by the Constitution.

(b) All entities subject to audit shall have the right to respond to findings and challenge determinations in federal court.

(c) All audit escalation rules shall be defined ex ante by Congress and shall not be subject to unilateral administrative expansion.

---

## SECTION 11. LIMITATIONS ON EXPANSION

(a) Audit escalation shall be limited to three tiers.

(b) Maximum retrospective audit reach shall be defined by statute and may not be exceeded.

(c) All audits shall terminate upon completion of statutory scope or expiration of applicable sunset provisions.

---

## SECTION 12. SUNSET AND REAUTHORIZATION

(a) Tier 2 and Tier 3 expansions shall expire unless reauthorized by Congress within a defined statutory period.

(b) Congress shall review the effectiveness of the AAES not less than once every five (5) years.

---

## SECTION 13. EFFECTIVE DATE

This Act shall take effect one hundred eighty (180) days after enactment.
